About the Guide de Bordeaux

Welcome to Bordeaux , a beautiful city located in the southwestern part of France. Known for its world-famous wines and UNESCO World Heritage-listed architecture, Bordeaux is a must-visit destination for travelers. Stroll along the Garonne River, admire the stunning architecture of the Place de la Bourse, and indulge in the local cuisine and wine. Whether you're interested in history, culture, or simply looking for a relaxing vacation, Bordeaux has something for everyone.

LES INCOURTOURNABLES DE BORDEAUX

1. Porte Cailhau

Built in the 15th century, this magnificent gate is one of the most well-preserved examples of Gothic architecture in the city. Admire the intricate carvings and decorative elements on the facade of the gate, and climb to the top for breathtaking views of the Garonne River and the surrounding city.

2. Grand Théâtre

Grand Théâtre de Bordeaux, an iconic opera house and cultural institution in the heart of the city. Built in the 18th century, this magnificent building features stunning neoclassical architecture, ornate decorations, and a grand entrance staircase. Today, the Grand Théâtre continues to host world-class opera, ballet, and theater performances, as well as a variety of other cultural events and exhibitions. Whether you're a lover of the arts or simply interested in experiencing the rich cultural heritage of Bordeaux, a visit is a must.

3. Place de la Bourse - Miroir d’eau

Welcome to Place de la Bourse and the Miroir d'Eau, two iconic landmarks in Bordeaux, France. The Place de la Bourse is a stunning square that features 18th-century architecture and a central fountain. It's a popular gathering spot for locals and tourists alike, and is surrounded by shops, restaurants, and cultural institutions. Just steps away from the Place de la Bourse, you'll find the Miroir d'Eau, the world's largest reflecting pool. This unique attraction is especially beautiful at night when the water reflects the lights of the surrounding buildings, creating a mesmerizing display. A visit to Place de la Bourse and the Miroir d'Eau is a must for anyone visiting Bordeaux.

4. Grosse cloche

Dating back to the 15th century, this iconic monument features a bell that was used to signal curfew and other important events throughout the city's history. Climb to the top of the tower for panoramic views of Bordeaux's charming old town and the surrounding neighborhoods. The Grosse Cloche is a must-visit destination for history buffs and anyone interested in exploring the rich cultural heritage of this beautiful city.
